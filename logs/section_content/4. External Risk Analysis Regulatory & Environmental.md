## Food Safety, Regulatory Compliance, and Environmental Risks

This section assesses the risks associated with food safety, regulatory compliance, and environmental impact, focusing on their potential effects on Zomato's operations, reputation, and financial stability. These risks are particularly pertinent given increasing regulatory scrutiny and consumer awareness of sustainability.

### Food Safety & Regulatory Compliance Risks

A significant risk involves hygiene standards and adherence to food safety regulations, particularly within Zomato's Hyperpure warehouse operations. A recent report by TheLogicalIndian.com highlighted hygiene concerns and questionable packing dates, raising alarms about potential non-compliance. The potential impacts of such issues are far-reaching. Reputational damage is a primary concern, as negative publicity surrounding hygiene lapses can erode customer trust and brand loyalty. Financially, confirmed non-compliance can lead to substantial fines, penalties, and legal repercussions from regulatory bodies. Furthermore, restaurants relying on Hyperpure may seek alternative suppliers if doubts arise regarding the integrity of Zomato's supply chain, leading to a loss of business. Operationally, the discovery of hygiene deficiencies could necessitate the suspension of warehouse activities for remediation, causing disruptions throughout the supply chain.

The likelihood of these risks materializing appears moderate to high, given that the reported hygiene concerns suggest an ongoing problem requiring immediate attention. The severity of the impact is also rated as moderate to high, contingent on the extent of the hygiene issues and the subsequent regulatory response. The incident reported by TheLogicalIndian.com in 2024 underscores the immediacy of this risk. The primary regulatory and compliance considerations revolve around adherence to stringent food safety regulations in India, which govern the handling, storage, and distribution of food products. While the provided search results do not detail Zomato's existing risk controls, it is expected that the company has implemented internal quality control measures and supplier audit procedures to mitigate these risks. A critical risk interdependency exists, as poor hygiene practices can directly contribute to foodborne illnesses, posing a threat to public health and potentially triggering lawsuits against Zomato and its partners.

### Climate Change & Environmental Risks

The environmental impact of Zomato's food delivery operations constitutes another significant risk area. This encompasses carbon emissions from delivery vehicles, packaging waste, and other factors contributing to climate change. The potential impacts are multifaceted. Reputational risks arise from negative perceptions among environmentally conscious consumers if Zomato is perceived as a major contributor to environmental degradation. Strategically, the company faces the risk of shifting consumer preferences towards more sustainable food options and delivery services. Financially, Zomato may encounter increased operating costs due to the implementation of carbon taxes or other environmental regulations aimed at curbing emissions and promoting sustainable practices.

The likelihood of these climate change-related risks materializing is considered high, given the increasing certainty of climate change impacts and the growing momentum behind environmental regulations. The severity of the impact is rated as medium to high in the long term, as the consequences of climate change intensify and consumer expectations for corporate sustainability increase. Key risk trends include growing consumer awareness of environmental issues and mounting pressure on companies to reduce their carbon footprint and adopt sustainable business practices. Regulatory and compliance considerations involve potential future environmental regulations pertaining to carbon emissions, packaging materials, and waste management, which could significantly impact Zomato's operations and costs.

Zomato's ESG Factsheet FY24 highlights the company's efforts in fostering sustainability and promoting social responsibility, indicating a proactive approach to mitigating environmental risks. However, the effectiveness of these measures in addressing the full scope of the company's environmental impact remains to be seen. A crucial risk interdependency exists, as climate change could disrupt agricultural production, leading to food shortages and price increases for restaurants utilizing Zomato's platform, thereby affecting the entire food delivery ecosystem.

Zomato's commitment to Environmental, Social, and Governance (ESG) principles is fundamental to their business strategy and risk management approach.
