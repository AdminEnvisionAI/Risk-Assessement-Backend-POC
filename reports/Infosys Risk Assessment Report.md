### Environmental, Social, and Governance (ESG) Risks

Infosys faces increasing scrutiny and potential risks related to its environmental, social, and governance (ESG) performance. Failure to meet evolving ESG standards and stakeholder expectations can lead to reputational damage, regulatory penalties, and reduced investor confidence. ### Cybersecurity Risks

As a technology company, Infosys faces significant and evolving cybersecurity risks. These risks include data breaches, malware attacks, ransomware incidents, phishing campaigns, and insider threats. Successful cyberattacks can result in financial losses, legal liabilities, reputational damage, and disruptions to operations. Protecting sensitive data, including customer data, intellectual property, and financial information, is paramount. Implementing robust cybersecurity controls, such as firewalls, intrusion detection systems, data encryption, and multi-factor authentication, is essential to mitigate these risks. Regular security assessments, vulnerability scanning, and penetration testing can help identify and address weaknesses in the company's security posture. A comprehensive incident response plan is crucial for effectively managing and recovering from security incidents. Security awareness training for employees is essential to reduce the risk of human error and social engineering attacks.

### Compliance Risks

Infosys operates in a complex regulatory environment and must comply with a wide range of laws and regulations globally. These include data privacy regulations (e.g., GDPR, CCPA), anti-corruption laws (e.g., FCPA, UK Bribery Act), labor laws, environmental regulations, and financial reporting requirements. Failure to comply with applicable laws and regulations can result in fines, sanctions, legal challenges, and reputational damage. Establishing a robust compliance program, including policies, procedures, training, and monitoring, is essential to mitigate compliance risks. Regular audits and risk assessments can help identify and address compliance gaps. Staying abreast of changes in the regulatory landscape is crucial for ensuring ongoing compliance.

### Operational Risks

Infosys faces operational risks related to its global operations, project execution, and service delivery. These risks include supply chain disruptions, project management failures, talent shortages, and business continuity challenges. Supply chain disruptions can impact the company's ability to deliver services to its clients. Project management failures can result in cost overruns, delays, and dissatisfied clients. Talent shortages can limit the company's growth and innovation potential. Business continuity challenges, such as natural disasters or pandemics, can disrupt operations and impact service delivery. Implementing robust operational controls, such as supply chain risk management, project management methodologies, talent management programs, and business continuity plans, is essential to mitigate these risks.

### Strategic Risks

Infosys faces strategic risks related to technological disruption, competition, and changing customer needs. Rapid technological advancements can render existing services and business models obsolete. Increased competition from established players and emerging startups can erode market share and profitability. Changing customer needs and preferences can require the company to adapt its offerings and strategies. Investing in research and development, fostering innovation, and developing a deep understanding of customer needs are crucial for mitigating strategic risks. Actively monitoring the competitive landscape and adapting to emerging trends is essential for maintaining a competitive advantage.

### Risk Interdependencies and Correlations

The various risk categories are interconnected and can influence each other. Cybersecurity risks can have financial, operational, and reputational impacts. ESG risks are increasingly correlated with financial performance and investor sentiment. Compliance risks are often linked to operational failures or inadequate controls. Understanding these interdependencies is crucial for effective risk management. A holistic approach to risk management that considers the interconnectedness of different risk categories is essential for mitigating overall risk exposure.
